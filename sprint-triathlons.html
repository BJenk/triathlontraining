Sprint Triathlons

Standard Sprint Triathlon Distance

  • Swim: 750M (0.47mi)
  • Bike: 20K (12.4mi)
  • Run: 5K (3.1mi)

Sprint triathlons grow in popularity each year. The sprint tends to be a fun distance for first-time triathletes, and it can also be a great primer for more experienced athletes.

(Note: sprint triathlon distances tend to vary greatly from one race to the next, but generally any distance less than an Olympic triathlon constitutes a sprint triathlon.)

Training for a Sprint Triathlon

Preparing for a sprint triathlon is mostly about ensuring you have all the fundamentals covered before race day. Although this race is shorter, participating athletes will see great benefits from following a periodized training plan and following a specially designed diet. In order to get the most out of your training, we recommend joining a local triathlon club or something similar.

Selecting a Race

The USA Triathlon website is an excellent resource for picking out a nearby race. To get the most out of your training, try to choose a race that is about 15 weeks away. Check out the Sprint Triathlon Training Plan in our shop!

Nutrition

People new to triathlon racing should not neglect any elements that could affect their performance, especially nutrition. In addition to fitness, a healthy diet during training and racing can improve the overall experience of a sprint triathlon. Just to give you some perspective: the average athlete will burn anywhere from 3,000 to 5,000 calories on race day. Planning your food intake around your training is crucial to feeling your best before, on, and even after race day.

Tools of Trade

In order to complete a triathlon, you only need a few accessories: a swim cap, road bicycle and helmet, and running shoes. Gear for beginners tends to emphasize more support, while gear for advanced triathletes tends to emphasize less weight in order to shave off time.
