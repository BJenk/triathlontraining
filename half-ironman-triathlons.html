Half Ironman Distance Triathlons

Standard Half Ironman Distance

  • Swim: 1.2 Mi
  • Bike: 56 Mi
  • Run: 13.1 Mi

The Half Ironman triathlon is almost as challenging as the full – but its less rigorous stats lend the race a more encouraging feeling of “I can do this.”

To prepare yourself for a race of this distance, expect to spend roughly 24 weeks working on periodized triathlon training [insert link to post]. In order to successfully take on the Half Ironman and adhere to a strict diet, we recommend getting involved with local training clubs – and doing some thorough research before even getting started.

Going the Distance

You should only complete the entire distance of a Half Ironman on race day. A good training plan will be timed so that you’ll build up to your peak performance level just in time for the actual race. Don’t underestimate the importance of training for this kind of distance – consult a coach or qualified trainer if you want to ensure you have healthy results (and don’t put yourself at risk for injury).

Selecting a Race

North America hosts roughly 34 Half Ironman triathlons each year. To make sure you have adequate time to train, it’s best to select a race 6 months in advance of race day. When you’re deciding on a race, consider these factors:

Considerations

  • Race rules/ Regulations
  • Estimated Temperatures
  • Course Map
  • Altitude

Think of the race as an investment: your contribution will determine your outcome. If you’re not excited about the race, you’ll have trouble completing the training for it – so invest yourself in a race you’ll really enjoy.

Nutrition

If you’re new to Half Ironman racing, don’t neglect any elements that could affect your performance, especially nutrition. On top of your fitness, sticking to a strictly tailored diet during training and racing can make or break your overall experience. Just to give you an idea of how your diet might be different: the average athlete consumes somewhere between 6,000 and 8,000 calories on the day of a Half Ironman race. Not surprisingly, the quality of those calories really matters. (Whole foods and clean eating will get you further than energy bars and high-carb diets, especially in the long run.)

Tools of Trade

In order to compete in a Half Ironman, a triathlete just needs a swim cap, road bicycle and helmet, and running shoes. Although most athletes will bring more gear than the basics, that really just depends on individual preferences. Generally, gear for people who are just starting out will be targeted towards more support. More advanced athletes’ gear will tend to involve less weight for faster performance.
